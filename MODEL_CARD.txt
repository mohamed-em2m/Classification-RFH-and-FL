1. Model Overview
This repository contains deep learning, graph-based, and traditional machine learning models developed to classify Reactive Follicular Hyperplasia (RFH) and Follicular Lymphoma (FL) using histopathological data.
The proposed framework integrates:
CNN-based image embeddings (AlexNet, VGG16, ResNet18),
Cell-level representations derived from CellViT++,
Spatial descriptors obtained via Cell Graph Neural Networks (Cell-GNN),
Morphometric nuclear features, and
Clinicopathological variables,
through a late-fusion multimodal architecture.
The system is designed as a research and proof-of-concept tool for computational pathology.

3. Model Performance Summary
ūüß© Morphometric Analysis
Quantitative nuclear morphometry demonstrated statistically significant differences between RFH and FL:

FL nuclei:
Larger nuclear area (p < 0.0051)
Greater perimeter (p < 0.0105)
Higher eccentricity (p < 0.0001)

RFH nuclei:
Higher circularity (p < 0.0026)
Mean optical density (OD):
No significant difference (p > 0.1614)
These findings indicate a clear morphometric distinction between benign and malignant follicular lesions.

4. Intended Use
Purpose: Assist research into RFH vs FL differentiation
Intended users: Pathologists, pathology researchers, AI developers

5. Limitations
Moderate dataset size
Limited number of external validation centres
Patch-based inference does not capture full WSI context
No evaluation on non-H&E stains or frozen sections

6. Potential Biases
Demographic and institutional bias
ROI selection bias due to expert annotations
Mitigated through colour normalisation, class weighting, and multicentre data

7. Ethical and Responsible AI Considerations
Outputs are assistive, not diagnostic
Interpretability ensured via Grad-CAM and SHAP
Data anonymisation and ethical approvals respected
Further large-scale validation required prior to clinical use
